01 — Flagship

Gum Arabic

A natural resin harvested from Acacia trees, treasured for centuries as a binder and stabilizer. Sudan currently dominates the world market, producing about 80% of available product — and our gum is widely recognized as the highest quality in the trade.

We supply both Hashab (Acacia senegal) and Talha (Acacia seyal) in a complete range of grades — from raw nodules to cleaned, hand-picked, and powdered forms — for food, pharmaceutical, cosmetic and industrial use.

  • Hashab Cleaned, Hand-Picked, Sifting
  • Hashab Dust & Cleaned Powder
  • Talha Dust & Talha Cleaned

02

Groundnut

Belonging to the pea and bean family, groundnut is enjoyed worldwide — raw, boiled, steamed or roasted, and as a key ingredient in confectionery and family meals.

Our Sudanese groundnuts are valued for their high nutritional density and consistent kernel size, making them a favorite of confectioners and snack producers in Europe, Asia and the Americas.

  • Calcium 90mg · Phosphorus 350mg per serving
  • Vitamin E and B-complex content
  • Calorific value 567 kcal
03

Peanut Oil

Known for its mild flavor and high smoke point, peanut oil is a staple in cuisines around the world. Its high monounsaturated fat content makes it a healthier choice than saturated oils, and it resists rancidity exceptionally well.

  • Aromatic roasted, refined, extra virgin / cold-pressed
  • Suitable for high-heat cooking and food processing
  • Long shelf life with natural stability
04

Sesame Seeds

Sesame seeds are highly valued for their high content of sesame oil — an oil exceptionally resistant to rancidity. They are a foundational ingredient in tahini, halvah, and countless culinary traditions across the Middle East, Asia, and Europe.

  • Ground into tahini for hummus and Middle Eastern sweets
  • Topping for breads, salads, stir-fries and confectionery
  • Source for premium sesame oil and Japanese gomasio
  • Used in margarine production across Europe
05

Hibiscus

Hibiscus sabdariffa produces deep red, fleshy calyces — the foundation of karkade, the famed Sudanese hibiscus tea. Beyond beverages, hibiscus is used in jams, spices, soups and traditional medicine.

  • Karkade — refreshing herbal tea
  • Jams, spice blends, soups and sauces
  • Traditional uses for appetite, digestion, respiratory comfort
  • Natural source of food coloring and antioxidants
06

Melon Seeds

Encased in a grayish-white shell with a soft, oval inner kernel — melon seeds are rich in protein, omega-3 fatty acids, vitamins and natural fats. They offer a nutty, sweet flavor and are popular as a roasted snack and culinary ingredient.

  • Consumed roasted as a premium snack
  • Used in traditional African and Middle Eastern dishes
  • Source of plant-based protein and healthy fats
07

Senna Pods

A traditional botanical with a long history of use as a natural laxative and herbal remedy. We supply both pods and leaves for the herbal medicine and pharmaceutical industries.

  • Useful in constipation and digestive support
  • Traditional remedy in herbal preparations
  • Available as pods, leaves or whole plant material
